Experiences

Texas DPS - Cyber security intern
June 11, 2017 - August 11, 2017

At the Texas Department of Public Safety (DPS), I worked in the cyber security division handling standard phishing and malware cases, as well as writing python scripts to automate much of the work in the office, such as scanning computers for certain malicious files, and analyzing malicious javascript files. In addition, I worked on implementing The Hive's (a security management system) API into our daily workflow to increase efficiency and automate many of the tasks. Finally, I helped the network security and dev ops division migrate firewalls from Palo Altos to Fortimails, as well as update the email security appliances used to protect the DPS.

Austin Spark League
July 2015 - August 2015

The Austin Spark League (ASL) was one of my first experiences working on a group programming project. I learned how to plan project workflow with my teammates, as well as effectively use git and github to simultaneously work on our projects. ASL focused on creating apps for the Pebble smartwatch, one of the first popular wrist watches that had capabilities similar to smartphones. The apps I developed for ASL include a visual probability simulator and an app that would supply random jokes,insults, and compliments.

Code@TACC
June 2015 - July 2015

One of my activities during my sophomore summer was attending a camp at the Texas Advanced Computing Center (TACC) at the Pickle campus of UT-Austin. There I had the chance to learn a lot about how electronics and computers really work, as well as experiment and program a raspberry pi. As part of the campI also got to see and use some of the most cutting edge technology, such as the UT sponsored super computer, and an experimental virtual reality show for the oculus rift.
